Acute Deterioration Response

Rapid clinical support when residents become unwell

When a care home resident becomes acutely unwell, the EnRICH team provides rapid clinical support to help determine the most appropriate care pathway. Our specialist geriatricians can assess residents on-site, provide clinical guidance, and help avoid unnecessary hospital admissions while ensuring residents receive the right care at the right time.

Older adults often present atypically with subtle signs of illness ‚Äì known as the geriatric syndromes. Rather than classic symptoms, they may show:

  • Delirium ‚Äì Sudden confusion, disorientation, or personality changes that may be mistaken for dementia
  • Falls ‚Äì New or increased falls, even without obvious injury, can signal underlying illness
  • Reduced mobility ‚Äì "Off legs", difficulty getting out of bed, or increased dependence
  • Incontinence ‚Äì New or worsening continence issues can indicate infection or other illness
  • "Not themselves" ‚Äì Subtle changes noticed by familiar carers who know the resident well

üîó Continuity of Care

A key strength of the EnRICH model is that the same geriatrician team providing proactive community care also delivers front-door ED reviews and inpatient care. This continuity enables rapid clinical decision-making informed by longitudinal knowledge of residents, more effective communication between care settings, and quicker, safer discharge planning.

üìû When to Contact EnRICH

Contact the EnRICH team when a resident shows signs of acute deterioration and you need specialist geriatric input. This includes sudden confusion, unexplained falls, reduced mobility, new symptoms, or when you're uncertain about the best care pathway. We can provide advice, arrange urgent review, or guide appropriate escalation.
