MDT Collaboration

Working together for better outcomes

The Power of Partnership

Effective care for older adults with complex needs requires collaboration across multiple disciplines and organisations. The EnRICH service works closely with a wide range of partners to ensure coordinated, person-centred care for care home residents.

"Providing proactive care for care home residents requires multidisciplinary team (MDT) working. MDT meetings provide structured opportunities for clinical review, shared decision-making, and coordinated care planning." — British Geriatrics Society / Enhanced Health in Care Homes Framework

By bringing together different perspectives and expertise, we can address the full range of residents' needs - medical, psychological, social, and functional - in a coordinated way.

Regular MDT Meetings

Structured multidisciplinary team meetings are at the heart of the EnRICH approach:

What Happens in MDT Meetings

  • Case discussion - Review of residents with complex needs or recent changes
  • Care planning - Developing and updating personalised care plans
  • Problem solving - Addressing challenges with input from multiple perspectives
  • Communication - Ensuring all team members have current information
  • Education - Sharing knowledge and learning from cases
  • Forward planning - Anticipating needs and preparing for changes

MDT meetings ensure that care is coordinated, comprehensive, and aligned with each resident's goals. They also help identify residents who might benefit from proactive intervention before a crisis occurs.

Benefits of MDT Working

Evidence shows that multidisciplinary collaboration leads to better outcomes:

  • Comprehensive assessment - Multiple perspectives identify more issues and opportunities
  • Coordinated care - Avoiding gaps and duplication in services
  • Faster response - Clear communication channels when problems arise
  • Reduced hospital admissions - More problems managed safely in the community
  • Better advance care planning - Shared understanding of residents' wishes
  • Staff satisfaction - Feeling supported in delivering complex care

Integrated Care System Working

Part of a Bigger Picture

EnRICH operates within the Cambridgeshire & Peterborough Integrated Care System (ICS), contributing to system-wide efforts to improve care for older people.

We work with:

  • Cambridge University Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust
  • Cambridgeshire & Peterborough NHS Foundation Trust (CPFT)
  • Primary Care Networks (PCNs)
  • East of England Ambulance Service
  • Local Authority social care teams
  • Voluntary sector organisations

This integrated approach ensures that care home residents benefit from the full range of services available across the health and care system, with smooth transitions between settings.

How EnRICH Connects the System

The EnRICH team acts as a bridge between care homes and the wider health system. By maintaining relationships with multiple partners and providing continuity across settings, we help ensure that care is coordinated and that residents don't fall through the gaps. When a resident needs input from another service, we can facilitate referrals and ensure effective handover.
