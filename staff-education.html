Staff Education & Support

Building confidence and capability in care home teams

Why Education Matters

Care home staff are at the frontline of caring for older adults with complex health needs. Building their confidence and capability through education and ongoing support is essential for delivering high-quality care and recognising when residents need specialist input.

"Improving knowledge and skills in geriatric medicine for all care home staff is essential to improve the quality of care for residents. Education should be tailored to the needs of the care home and delivered in accessible formats." — British Geriatrics Society Care Homes Group

The EnRICH team works alongside care home staff, providing regular education sessions, clinical discussions, and real-time support to enhance the care delivered to residents.

Our Education & Support Services

We provide comprehensive education and support to care home teams:

Regular Engagement

Scheduled teaching sessions tailored to each care home's learning needs and priorities.

Case Review

Clinical discussion of complex residents and reflective learning from outcomes.

Escalation Pathways

Clear guidance on when and how to seek specialist advice or urgent review.

Clinical Policies

Development of practical guidelines for common clinical scenarios.

Specialist Access

Rapid access to geriatrician advice when staff face challenging clinical situations.

Skills Development

Building competence in frailty recognition, NEWS2, and clinical assessment.

Key Learning Topics

Our education sessions cover essential topics for care home staff:

  • Frailty recognition - Understanding and identifying frailty using the Clinical Frailty Scale
  • Delirium awareness - Recognising confusion as a sign of illness, not just dementia progression
  • Falls prevention - Risk factors, assessment, and practical prevention strategies
  • End of life care - Supporting residents and families during the final stages of life
  • Medication safety - Recognising side effects, interactions, and when to raise concerns
  • Advance care planning - Having difficult conversations and documenting wishes

Benefits of Education

What Staff Education Achieves

  • Improved recognition - Earlier identification of deteriorating residents
  • Confident decision-making - Knowing when to escalate and when to manage in-house
  • Better communication - Using clinical language that facilitates effective handover
  • Reduced hospital transfers - Managing more conditions safely in the care home
  • Enhanced resident experience - Care that aligns with individual preferences
  • Staff satisfaction - Greater confidence and professional development

Clear Escalation Pathways

We work with each care home to establish clear, practical escalation pathways:

When You're Concerned About a Resident

Recognise change → Document observations → Check ReSPECT form → Contact EnRICH or GP → Implement advice

Our team provides guidance on the appropriate level of escalation - from simple advice calls through to urgent on-site assessment or hospital transfer when needed.

Education in EnRICH

Education is embedded throughout the EnRICH model. During every care home visit, we take opportunities to discuss clinical cases, share knowledge, and build relationships with care home staff. This ongoing engagement creates a foundation of trust that enables effective collaboration when residents become unwell. We also offer formal teaching sessions on specific topics by arrangement.
