Comprehensive Geriatric Assessment (CGA)

Gold standard care for older adults

What is CGA?

Comprehensive Geriatric Assessment (CGA) is a structured multidisciplinary approach offering gold standard care for older adults. It is evidence-based and increasingly recognised for its role in improving quality of care for older people across various healthcare settings.

Rather than being viewed as an isolated assessment of need, CGA goes beyond a routine medical assessment and incorporates all physical, psychological, functional, social and environmental factors. These are then used to develop an integrated holistic care plan that is individualised to the person.

"A multidimensional, multidisciplinary process that identifies a person's medical, social and functional needs and the development of an integrated and coordinated care plan to address these needs." — British Geriatrics Society

The Six Domains of CGA

CGA consists of six key domains, each essential for creating a comprehensive care plan:

1. Physical

Medical history, frailty syndromes, long-term condition management, polypharmacy, nutrition, pain management, and falls risk.

2. Psychological

Cognition, mood, delirium risk, mental health conditions, and emotional well-being.

3. Functional

Mobility, activities of daily living (ADLs/IADLs), gait stability, and rehabilitation potential.

4. Social

Social support, carer needs, isolation, spiritual/religious beliefs, and financial concerns.

5. Environmental

Home safety, accessibility, community services, and technology aids.

6. Future Wishes

Advance care planning, treatment preferences, resuscitation status, and long-term care decisions.

Why CGA Matters: The Evidence

CGA has been shown to improve patient outcomes and optimise healthcare services across various settings:

  • In primary care: CGA helps identify at-risk individuals living with frailty and supports early interventions, reducing hospital admissions
  • In community settings: CGA has been linked to reduction in physical frailty and unplanned hospital admissions
  • In acute hospital settings: Significant reductions in falls, admissions to long-term care facilities, and delirium incidence
  • In emergency departments: Lower admission rates, reduced functional impairment, and fewer re-admissions
  • In surgical settings: Better recovery, lower delirium incidence, and reduced admission to long-term care

Who Performs CGA?

CGA is a multidisciplinary approach involving various professionals, each contributing unique expertise:

  • Medical experts: Consultant Geriatricians, GPs with specialist interest in older people and frailty
  • Nursing staff: Monitor health changes, skin integrity, nutrition and support holistic care
  • Allied Health Professionals: Physiotherapists, Occupational Therapists, Speech and Language Therapists, Dietitians
  • Pharmacists: Optimise medication prescribing and review polypharmacy risks
  • Social Workers & Care Coordinators: Address social determinants, care needs, and community support

CGA in EnRICH

Through the EnRICH service, our specialist geriatricians provide comprehensive geriatric assessments directly in care homes. This means residents receive gold-standard assessment without the need to travel to hospital, in a familiar and comfortable environment. Our assessments are targeted to high-risk residents including previous frequent presenters and GP-identified complex cases.
